Hearing & Equipment

1. Why can't I hear Ultra Low frequency sounds?

Ultra-low frequencies (typically below 60Hz) require high-quality playback equipment. Regular smartphone speakers or consumer-grade headphones may not be able to reproduce such low frequencies. For the best training experience, we strongly recommend using high-quality studio monitor headphones or external audio equipment.

2. Why can't I hear High frequency sounds?

This is usually a normal physiological phenomenon. Human hearing range naturally deteriorates with age, particularly in the high-frequency range. Additionally, prolonged noise exposure, genetic factors, and the health of hair cells in the cochlea all affect the highest frequencies you can hear.
