Hearing and Devices

1. Why can’t I hear ultra-low frequency sounds?

Ultra-low frequencies (typically below 60Hz) have high requirements for playback devices. Regular phone speakers or consumer-grade earphones may not be able to reproduce such low frequencies. For the best training experience, we strongly recommend using high-quality studio headphones or external audio equipment.

2. Why can’t I hear high-frequency sounds?

This is usually a normal physiological phenomenon. The human hearing range naturally declines with age, particularly in the high-frequency range. Additionally, long-term noise exposure, genetic factors, and the health of hair cells in the cochlea can affect the highest frequencies you can hear.
